🚚 Operations & Supply Chain Careers in India: E-commerce, Logistics, and Manufacturing 2026

📅 May 2026 ⏱ 11 min read 🏷️ #OperationsJobs #NonTechJobs

India’s logistics and supply chain sector is undergoing a massive transformation, driven by e-commerce giants (Amazon, Flipkart), quick-commerce (Blinkit, Zepto), and manufacturing push (PLI schemes). Operations professionals are the backbone of these industries – they ensure goods move efficiently, costs are controlled, and customers are satisfied.

Key roles in operations & supply chain

  • Warehouse Operations Manager: Oversee inventory, staffing, and productivity. Salary: ₹5–12 LPA.
  • Logistics Coordinator / Supply Chain Analyst: Plan routes, track shipments, optimise costs. ₹3.5–8 LPA.
  • Procurement Specialist: Source raw materials/vendors. ₹5–10 LPA.
  • Inventory Planner: Forecast demand, prevent stockouts. ₹4–9 LPA.
  • Last-Mile Delivery Manager: Manage delivery partners and SLAs. ₹6–14 LPA.
  • Supply Chain Consultant: Advise firms on efficiency. ₹10–20 LPA.

Certifications and skills

While a B.Com or BBA is common, certifications like CSCP (APICS), Six Sigma Green Belt, or SAP MM add significant value. Excel (pivot tables, VLOOKUP) is non-negotiable; knowledge of ERP systems (SAP, Oracle NetSuite) or WMS is a plus. Many operations professionals start as interns at Delhivery, Ecom Express, or FMCG companies.

💡 Pro tip: Operations roles often require working in shifts or field locations. If you enjoy problem-solving and hands-on work, this field offers rapid growth – many plant heads and regional ops directors earn ₹25–40 LPA.

How to get hired

Target companies like Amazon India (operations pathways program), Flipkart, Rivigo, or traditional manufacturers (Tata, Mahindra). Prepare for case-study interviews: e.g., “How would you reduce dispatch time by 20%?” Freshers typically start as operations executives (₹3–5 LPA) and can double salary in 3 years.
